Description

Momo is a type of steamed dumpling with some form of filling, most commonly chicken (traditionally yak, but often chicken and goat) and it is originally from Tibet. Momo has become a delicacy in Nepal and Tibetan communities in Bhutan, as well the Indian regions of Darjeeling, Ladakh and Sikkim.

Ingredients

  • ALL PURPOSE FLOUR
  • OIL
  • SALT & PEPPER
  • LEAN GROUND MEAT
  • ONIONS , TOMATOES & GARLIC
  • SPICES
  • NUTMEG

Steps

  1. Dough: In a large bowl combine flour, oil, salt and water.
  2. Mix well, knead until the dough becomes homogeneous in texture, about 8-10 minute.
  3. Cover and let stand for at least 30 minute.
  4. Knead well again before making wrappers.
  5. Filling: In a large bowl combine all filling ingredients.
  6. Mix well, adjust for seasoning with salt and pepper.
  7. Fill dough with meat and steam for 10 minutes
